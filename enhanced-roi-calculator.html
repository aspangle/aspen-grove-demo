Calculation Methodology

Our ROI calculator uses a sophisticated model based on data from hundreds of legal practices using our platform:

  • Efficiency Gain: We apply a variable efficiency rate based on your document volume. Practices typically see 40-60% time savings with our AI tools.
  • Additional Capacity: Calculated as your current volume multiplied by our efficiency factor, which accounts for diminishing returns at higher volumes.
  • Revenue Impact: The product of your additional capacity, hours per document, and billable rate.
  • ROI Percentage: Annual revenue increase divided by the annual subscription cost of our platform.

Results may vary based on practice area, document complexity, and implementation factors.

How to Use the ROI Calculator

1

Select Your Role

Start by choosing the view that best matches your perspective:

  • Legal Professional: Focus on billable hours, case capacity, and revenue impact
  • Business Owner: Focus on operational costs, resource optimization, and ROI timeline
2

Adjust the Monthly Document Volume

Drag the slider left or right to set the number of documents your organization processes each month. You can adjust from 10 to 300 documents.

Set Hours per Document

Adjust the slider to indicate how many hours are typically spent processing each document. Range is from 2 to 10 hours.

4

Enter Your Rate

Move the slider to set your hourly rate, which represents either billable rate (for legal professionals) or operational cost (for business owners), from $150 to $500 per hour.

5

View Your Potential Results

As you adjust the sliders, the calculator automatically updates to show your potential improvements with Aspen Grove AI. Watch for animated changes in the right column.

6

Compare with Industry Benchmarks

Note the benchmark figures below each input to see how your current metrics compare to industry standards.

7

Keyboard Navigation

You can also use your keyboard to adjust the sliders:

  • Tab key to move between sliders
  • Arrow right/up to increase values
  • Arrow left/down to decrease values
